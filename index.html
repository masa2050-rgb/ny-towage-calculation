Model Assumptions

TEU, NRT, and GRT Conversion

  • Feeder/Standard (< 5,100 TEU): Baseline LOA ~965 ft. Uses a 5.2 multiplier for NRT and 12.0 for GRT.
  • Neo-Panamax/ULCV (5,100 - 14,500 TEU): LOA interpolates to ~1,200 ft. NRT multiplier scales down to 4.8 - 5.0.
  • Megamax/SLCV (> 14,500 TEU): LOA pushes towards 1,300 ft. Heavy deck-

Vessel Classification

  • LOA ≥ 1,165 ft → SLCV/MLCV: 4 tugs (with thruster), 5 tugs (without)
  • LOA ≥ 997 ft → ULCV: 3 tugs (with thruster), 4 tugs (without)
  • LOA < 997 ft → Standard: 2 tugs

Escort Billing & Runtime Billing Policy

  • Post-Sept 2025: Flat $1,000/hr regardless of escort type
  • Standard zone transit for these terminals is 2.50 hrs inbound (Bayonne, NY, Elizabeth/Newark, Red Hook)
  • Legacy Standard: $1,890/hr · Legacy Tethered: $3,145/hr
  • Minimum billed time: 2.0 hours per tug (rounded to nearest 0.5 hr).
  • Waived/Absorbed policy gives 0.0hr outbound credit in dual-service escort+flat docking cases

Fuel Surcharge

  • Moran uses the NY Harbor No. 2 Diesel index for fuel pricing
  • Base fuel price: $2.00/gal — no surcharge at or below this level
  • Each $0.10 increment above base adds $15 per tug
  • Applied to all physically dispatched tugs (not just docking tugs)

KVK Transit Anomaly

  • Port Liberty New York + Elizabeth/Newark (Zones 10 & 11) require Kill Van Kull transit
  • One escort tug is released before docking → fewer docking tugs
  • Creates "Escort Only" tug classification for billing purposes
  • Dual-service route can use Waived/Absorbed policy to credit outbound transit (0.0 hours toward escort billable runtime)
