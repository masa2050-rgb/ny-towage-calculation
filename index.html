Baseline Assumptions

Terminal

Drives: Actual Escort Time and Runtime

Assumption:

  • Port Liberty Bayonne: Sets Actual Escort Time to 0.50 hrs and base Zone Runtime to 2.50 hrs (reduces to 1.25 hrs if waived).
  • Port Liberty New York: Sets Actual Escort Time to 1.00 hr, base Zone Runtime to 2.50 hrs, and triggers strict KVK protocols (shifts 1 dual-service tug to an "Escort Only" tug). Does not waive runtime.
  • APM Elizabeth: Sets Actual Escort Time to 1.00 hr, base Zone Runtime to 2.50 hrs (reduces to 1.25 hrs if waived), and triggers strict KVK protocols.

Direction

Drives: Runtime-waiver

Assumption:

  • Inbound (Arrival): Qualifies for the runtime waiver (terminal dependent). Halves the base Zone Runtime (e.g., 2.50 hrs → 1.25 hrs) for Escort+Docking tugs because the return leg is waived once they transition to docking.
  • Outbound (Departure): No waiver applied. Escorting tugs are billed the full round-trip Zone Time (e.g., 2.50 hrs).

Length Overall (LOA)

Drives: Docking and Escort Units

Assumption:

  • < 850 ft (Standard): Assigns 2 Docking tugs. Escort services are not mandatory (0 Escort units).
  • 850 ft – 1164 ft (ULCV): Assigns 3 Docking tugs and triggers mandatory escorting (3 Escort units).
  • ≥ 1165 ft (SLCV/MLCV): Assigns 4 Docking tugs and 4 mandatory Escort units.

Net Register Tonnage (NRT)

Drives: +40k NRT Fee

Assumption:

  • ≤ 40,000 NRT: $0 size premium.
  • > 40,000 NRT: Adds exactly $1,400 to the invoice for every "Docking" line item assigned to the job.

Variables Assumptions

Adj: "Docking" Line Items

When to adjust:

  • Maximum Fleet Limit: Total physical tugs are hard-capped at 5 units.
  • Inoperable Bow Thruster: The Port Authority of NY & NJ guidelines dictate adding one (+1) additional docking tug for vessels without an operable bow thruster.
  • Adverse Weather / Ice Conditions: Safety guidelines require the assignment of one (+1) extra docking tug during harsh weather or ice conditions.

Adj: Escort + Docking & Adj: "Escort Only" Services

When to adjust:

  • PLB (USNYC01) Runtime Exceptions: There are operational exceptions where Moran NY utilizes "Escort Only" tugs instead of combined "Escort + Docking" services. You must manually decrease the dual service variable and increase the "Escort Only" variable to reflect this.
  • Small Vessels in the Kill Van Kull Transit: Per harbor regulations, vessels ≥ 850 ft require mandatory tug escorts. While vessels < 850 ft (Standard) default to 0 escort units, Moran NY uses "Escort Only" tugs when these smaller vessels transit restricted waterways like the Kill Van Kull to reach APM Elizabeth or Port Liberty New York.

Actual Escort Time (hrs)

When to adjust:

  • Operational Delays: Manually increase or decrease this value over the baseline assumptions to account for real-world variables such as heavy waterway traffic and weather delays.

Billing Formulas

Docking

Formula: Base Cost = Docking Rate × "Docking" Line Items

Assumption: Base tug dispatch is determined by Length Overall (LOA). 2 tugs for <850', 3 tugs for ≥850', and 4 tugs for ≥1165'.

+40K NRT Fee

Formula: Size Cost = If NRT > 40,000, add $1,400 × "Docking" Line Items

Assumption: This is an additional flat premium added per docking line item when the Net Register Tonnage exceeds the threshold.

Fuel Surcharge

Formula: Fuel Cost = ⌈(Fuel Price - 2.00) / 0.10⌉ × $15 × Total Physical Tugs

Assumption: Calculated per physical tug using a $2.00 base. Every $0.10 increment (rounded up) adds a $15 surcharge.

Escort

Formula: Escort Cost = Escort Rate × Billed Hours (Actual Escort Time + Runtime) × Total Escorting Tugs

Assumption: Billed Hours is rounded up to the nearest 0.5hrs increment and is subject to a 2.0-hour minimum billing per tug.

Per harbor regulations, vessels 850 feet LOA and larger require mandatory tug escorts to safely transit restricted waterways like the Kill Van Kull (Zone 11), whereas smaller vessels do not.
